# General Ledger Architecture Report

## Architecture Review
The General Ledger Domain has been successfully implemented and integrated with the Accounting Platform. It ensures that the General Ledger serves as the immutable book of records for all financial events generated by the business domain. The business domains are completely shielded from interacting directly with the ledger.

## Domain Model
- **Ledger**: The root ledger definition containing currency settings.
- **LedgerAccount**: Defines accounts bound to a Ledger.
- **LedgerPosting**: An immutable entry resulting from a posted journal line.
- **PostingBatch**: An aggregation representing the process of turning a Draft Journal Entry into immutable ledger records.
- **PostingValidator**: Ensures the fundamental rules of accounting are met (double-entry balancing, closed/open period checks).
- **PostingPolicy**: A domain service to assert if an entry can be posted using validators.
- **PostJournalCoordinator**: A Process Coordinator wrapper for triggering ledger postings.

## Dependency Review
- **Isolated from Business Logic**: The General Ledger knows absolutely nothing about Human Capital, Sales, Procurement, or Inventory.
- **Integration with Accounting**: It securely accepts pre-validated \`JournalEntry\` payloads produced by the Accounting Platform.
- **Integration with Master Data**: It utilizes Fiscal Calendar structures for period validation without circular dependencies.

## Posting Flow Diagram
1. The Accounting Engine holds a DRAFT \`JournalEntry\`.
2. The Process Coordinator \`PostJournalCoordinator\` invokes \`PostingApplicationService\`.
3. Validation:
   - Ensures Journal has not been posted previously.
   - Ensures Debits = Credits.
   - Ensures the applicable \`FiscalPeriod\` is OPEN.
4. If valid, \`PostingBatch\` creates \`LedgerPosting\` elements for each \`JournalLine\`.
5. The Batch is saved, creating immutable records in the Ledger.
6. The source \`JournalEntry\` status is transitioned to POSTED.
